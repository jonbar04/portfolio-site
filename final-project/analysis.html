Analysis


Although it was a small sample size, it's worth looking through all the answers everyone gave a bit more in-depth.
The interviews page only contains brief summaries of the interviews, so here is all of the little pieces of data gathered through the interview process.


Basic Takeaways


2/3 people have been collecting for years.
The items collected were plushies, albums, and video games.
2/3 people said they would part with their most valuable possesion.
All said price doesn't really matter that much.


My Thoughts


Although everyone said similar yet different things when it came to the subject, it was interesting how the medium of their collections impacted their thinking. Something like a computer or game is easy to replace and upgrade, while plushies or music can be replaced if broken or torn, doesn't warrant replacement the same way the former does. Those things are tools and usually become out of date, warranting an upgrade at some point.

I find it interesting that everyone said price isn't really an important aspect. Yes, the sentiment of something or just the fact that it's been obtained is what really matters, but I'd expect money to matter more when it came to something's value.

A lot of the differences in general came from the different things they collect. In an era where companies are leaning more heavily on digital distribution, you could argue whether or not someone truly "owns" something, which reflects in the way people collect certain things. Some things are also just more accessible, with more ways to acquire them, including less-than-ethical methods such as pirating digital media.

Though at the end of the day, these people collect what they love. It doesn't matter what it is or how much they have, because it's their passion.
